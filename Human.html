Human Causes of Climate Change

Major Human Causes of Climate Change

  • Burning of Fossil Fuels

    Over the last century, burning of fossil fuels like coal and oil has increased the concentration of atmospheric carbon dioxide (CO2). This increase happens because the coal or oil burning process combines carbon with oxygen in the air to make CO2.

  • Deforestation

    Removing trees from forests reduces the number of plants that can absorb carbon dioxide from the atmosphere, leading to higher levels of this greenhouse gas.

  • Waste Management

    Improper waste disposal, especially in landfills, produces methane, a potent greenhouse gas that contributes to climate change.
